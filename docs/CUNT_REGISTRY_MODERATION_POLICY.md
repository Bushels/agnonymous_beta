# C.U.N.T. Registry Moderation Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-15

## Status

This is the internal operating policy for the first production version. It is not legal advice. Canadian counsel should review the registry, submission terms, notice process, privacy treatment, and retention rules before real allegations are accepted or published.

## Access Boundary

- The entire registry requires a signed-in account with a verified email.
- Anonymous and unverified visitors cannot read approved reports, comments, votes, watches, accused-party details, or evidence.
- A report owner may see their own pending report.
- Administrators may see all pending, approved, rejected, and deleted reports needed for moderation.

## Submission Gate

A report must contain:

- The submitter's verified account and public username.
- The accused party's identifying information.
- A clear description of what happened.
- Location, item involved, and claimed financial loss.
- At least one supporting image or document.
- A good-faith confirmation that the submission is accurate to the reporter's knowledge and does not expose unrelated personal information.

Submission creates a pending report. Pending reports are not visible to other registry users.

## Moderator Checklist

Before approval, confirm:

1. The report describes a specific agricultural transaction or attempted transaction.
2. The named party can be distinguished from similarly named people or businesses.
3. The evidence supports the central allegation and is not merely an unsupported screenshot of the allegation itself.
4. Dates, amounts, location, and transaction details are internally consistent.
5. The report does not expose bank numbers, government identifiers, unrelated addresses, children, medical information, or other unnecessary personal data.
6. The wording distinguishes documented facts from the reporter's opinion or inference.
7. The submission is not an obvious duplicate, personal dispute unrelated to agriculture, retaliation, spam, or doxxing.
8. Any material uncertainty is resolved with the reporter before publication.

Approval publishes the report to verified registry users and writes an immutable moderation audit record.

## Rejection

Reject a report when evidence is missing, the claim cannot be tied to the named party, the submission is materially inconsistent, privacy exposure is excessive, or the subject matter is outside the registry. A rejection reason is mandatory and is stored in the moderation audit trail.

## Challenge, Correction, and Takedown

- Provide a clear channel for the named party to challenge a report or submit a response.
- Temporarily hide a challenged report when identity, evidence authenticity, or a material fact is credibly disputed.
- Preserve the original report, evidence, moderation record, and later corrections for internal review. Do not silently rewrite the historical record.
- Correct or remove content promptly when evidence shows it is materially false, identifies the wrong party, exposes unnecessary private data, or violates law or platform policy.
- Record every approval, rejection, correction, hiding decision, restoration, and permanent takedown with the administrator, time, reason, and affected report.

## Administrator Routine

- Review the moderation queue before any report can publish.
- Never approve a report solely because evidence was uploaded.
- Do not download or redistribute evidence outside the moderation purpose.
- Use the least identifying information needed for the registry's protective purpose.
- Escalate threats, extortion, forged evidence, court documents, or uncertain legal demands to counsel before acting beyond temporary hiding and preservation.

## Pre-Launch Legal Gate

Do not invite real allegations until counsel has reviewed:

- Defamation and intermediary liability exposure.
- Privacy and consent obligations for accused-party information.
- Terms of submission and reporter attestations.
- Notice, response, appeal, correction, and takedown procedure.
- Evidence retention and deletion schedule.
- Law-enforcement and litigation-hold handling.
